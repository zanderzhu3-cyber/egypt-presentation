Challenges & Weaknesses
Five recurring problems across 3,000 years

⚔️ 1. Foreign Invasions

Sea Peoples (c. 1177 BCE) → Assyrian sack (671 BCE) → Persian conquest (525 BCE) → Alexander (332 BCE). Each time: cultural revival.

🌊 2. Nile Flood Variability

Low flood = famine. High flood = crop damage. Nileometers at Elephantine measured levels → tax rates adjusted accordingly.

⛓️ 3. Social Inequality

80%+ farmers bore heavy labor-tax. 90% of wealth controlled by 10% (priests, nobles). Still: more social mobility than Mesopotamia or Greece.

💰 4. Economic Vulnerability

No coins until Greek conquest (~300 BCE). Barter economy. State granaries functioned as de facto banks.

📦 5. Resource Import Dependence

No local timber (needed Lebanese cedar). Copper from Sinai, tin from Afghanistan. Trade disruption = construction halts.
